Who Am I?

Hi I'm Lauri Peterson I am a political scientist working on environmental politics and climate governance.

I am a Doctoral Candidate at the Department of Government at Uppsala University. I received my MA from the Central European University in 2013.

My research has been published in World Development, the Journal of Human Rights and the Environment and Social Indicators Research.

I enjoy hiking and photography.

Bureaucratic Politics and the Allocation of Climate Finance

World Development

117, 72-97
  • The paper underscores the importance of intra-governmental factors in the allocation of climate finance.
  • Lower income countries are more likely to be selected when the donor country’s ministry of development is leading on allocation decisions.
  • Wealthier developing countries receive more financing when the donor’s ministry of development is leading on allocation decisions.
  • UNFCCC allies are more likely to be selected when a donor country’s ministry of environment is involved in allocations decisions.
  • Non-allied countries within the UNFCCC negotiations are more likely to be penalized when the donor’s ministry of environment is leading.

Human rights and the clean development mechanism: lessons learned from three case studies

Journal of Human Rights and the Environment

8 (1), 51–71

This article analyses the human rights implications of projects under the Clean Development Mechanism (CDM) of the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change (UNFCCC). While the CDM is likely to expire in the near future, the experience gained should be used to inform the rules of the new mechanism to be established under the 2015 Paris Agreement. We argue that the CDM and the new mechanism, as international organizations under the guidance of UNFCCC member states, should apply the UN Guiding Principles on Business and Human Rights. Based on the experience drawn from three case studies (two hydro power projects in Barro Blanco, Panama, and Bujagali, Uganda, and one geothermal energy project in Olkaria, Kenya), we show that CDM projects, while in formal compliance with CDM rules, can lead to a number of human rights infringements. We conclude with a number of recommendations on how to achieve a greater recognition of human rights in the new mechanism under the Paris Agreement.
